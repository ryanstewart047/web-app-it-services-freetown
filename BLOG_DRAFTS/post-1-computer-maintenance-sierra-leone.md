## Why Computer Maintenance Matters in Sierra Leone

Sierra Leone's climate and infrastructure create specific challenges for computer owners:

- **Power fluctuations** can damage internal components without warning
- **Dust accumulation** is accelerated by our environment and unpaved roads
- **High humidity** can cause corrosion and electrical issues
- **Heat** makes cooling systems work harder, leading to faster wear

Regular maintenance addresses these challenges before they become expensive repair problems. A computer that receives proper care can last 5-7 years, while a neglected one might need major repairs within 2-3 years.

## Physical Cleaning: Your First Line of Defense

### External Cleaning (Weekly)

Your computer's exterior collects dust, dirt, and grime that can work their way inside. Here's how to clean it safely:

**What You'll Need:**
- Soft, lint-free cloth
- Isopropyl alcohol (70% or higher)
- Cotton swabs
- Compressed air (if available)

**Step-by-Step Process:**

1. **Power off completely** and unplug your device
2. **Wipe the screen** gently with a dry microfiber cloth using circular motions
3. **Clean the keyboard** by turning it upside down and gently shaking to remove debris
4. **Disinfect surfaces** using a cloth dampened (not wet) with isopropyl alcohol
5. **Clean ports** carefully with cotton swabs to remove dust buildup
6. **Wipe the exterior** including the bottom vents where dust accumulates most

**Important:** Never spray liquid directly on your computer. Always apply to your cloth first.

### Internal Cleaning (Every 3-6 Months)

Internal dust buildup is one of the leading causes of overheating in Freetown's dusty environment. While we recommend professional cleaning for this, here's what the process involves:

- Opening the computer case or laptop bottom panel
- Using compressed air to remove dust from fans and heat sinks
- Cleaning air intake and exhaust vents
- Checking for any signs of corrosion or damage
- Reapplying thermal paste if needed

**When to Seek Professional Help:**
- Your computer frequently overheats or shuts down unexpectedly
- Fans make loud grinding or rattling noises
- You're uncomfortable opening your device
- Your computer is still under warranty (opening it yourself may void warranty)

## Software Maintenance: Keeping Your System Healthy

### Operating System Updates

Many people in Freetown skip Windows updates due to slow internet or data costs, but this is risky:

**Why Updates Matter:**
- Security patches protect against viruses and malware
- Performance improvements make your computer run faster
- Bug fixes prevent system crashes
- Compatibility updates ensure software works properly

**How to Manage Updates on Slow Connections:**

1. Schedule updates during off-peak hours when internet is faster
2. Use a wired connection if available (more stable than WiFi)
3. Update critical security patches first, defer feature updates
4. Visit internet cafes with fast connections for major updates
5. Consider our shop - we can update your system while you wait

### Antivirus and Security

In Sierra Leone, we see many computers infected with malware from:
- Pirated software
- Shared USB drives
- Suspicious downloads
- Phishing emails

**Free Protection Options:**
- Windows Defender (built into Windows 10/11)
- Avast Free Antivirus
- AVG Free

**Best Practices:**
- Run a full system scan weekly
- Keep your antivirus updated
- Scan all USB drives before opening files
- Don't disable your antivirus to save resources
- Be cautious with free software downloads

### Disk Cleanup and Optimization

Over time, temporary files, cache, and unnecessary data slow down your computer. Here's a monthly maintenance routine:

**Disk Cleanup (Monthly):**
1. Press Windows + R, type "cleanmgr" and press Enter
2. Select your main drive (usually C:)
3. Check boxes for Temporary files, Recycle Bin, Downloads
4. Click "Clean up system files" for additional options
5. Let it run - this might take 10-30 minutes

**Disk Defragmentation (Monthly for HDD only):**
- Windows 10/11 do this automatically
- For older systems: Search "Defragment" in Start menu
- Run it overnight as it takes time
- Skip this if you have an SSD - it's unnecessary and harmful

### Browser Maintenance

Your internet browser can slow down significantly over time:

**Quick Browser Cleanup:**
1. Clear cache and cookies monthly
2. Remove unused extensions
3. Update to the latest version
4. Consider using Chrome or Edge for better performance in low-bandwidth situations

## Power Protection: Critical in Sierra Leone

Power issues are the #1 cause of computer damage we see at our repair shop. Here's how to protect your investment:

### Understanding Power Risks

**Common Power Problems in Freetown:**
- Sudden outages (can corrupt data and damage components)
- Voltage spikes when power returns
- Brownouts (low voltage that can damage power supplies)
- Fluctuating voltage during generator transitions

### Protection Solutions

**Level 1: Surge Protector (Minimum)**
- Cost: Le 100,000 - 200,000
- Protects against voltage spikes
- Replace every 2-3 years (they wear out)
- Look for models with indicator lights

**Level 2: Voltage Stabilizer (Recommended)**
- Cost: Le 300,000 - 500,000
- Regulates voltage fluctuations
- Especially important for expensive computers
- Choose one rated 20-30% above your computer's power consumption

**Level 3: UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply) (Best)**
- Cost: Le 800,000 - 1,500,000+
- Battery backup provides 5-30 minutes of runtime
- Allows safe shutdown during outages
- Regulates voltage automatically
- Essential for businesses and important work

**Choosing a UPS:**
- Calculate your computer's wattage (usually 300-500W for desktops, 60-90W for laptops)
- Buy a UPS rated at least 25% higher
- Brands available in Freetown: APC, CyberPower, Eaton
- Replace batteries every 2-3 years

### Safe Shutdown Practices

When power goes out:
1. Save all open documents immediately
2. Close programs properly (don't just force shutdown)
3. Shut down Windows normally
4. Unplug during extended outages (lightning risk)
5. Wait 2-3 minutes after power returns before turning on

## Heat Management in Tropical Climates

Freetown's heat and humidity are tough on computers. Overheating causes:
- Reduced performance (thermal throttling)
- System crashes and freezes
- Shortened component lifespan
- Permanent hardware damage

### Cooling Solutions

**Laptop Cooling:**
- Use a cooling pad (Le 150,000-300,000)
- Never use on bed, couch, or soft surfaces
- Ensure bottom vents are unobstructed
- Keep at least 6 inches clearance behind screen vents

**Desktop Cooling:**
- Position away from windows and direct sunlight
- Maintain 6-12 inches clearance on all sides
- Clean dust from vents monthly
- Consider adding case fans if overheating persists

**Room Temperature Tips:**
- Use fan or AC when possible
- Avoid computer use during hottest hours (1-4 PM)
- Shut down (not sleep) when not in use for extended periods
- Monitor temperature using free software like HWMonitor

### Warning Signs of Overheating

Seek professional help if you experience:
- Unexpected shutdowns
- Blue screen errors
- Loud fan noise constantly
- Computer hot to touch even when idle
- Graphical glitches or artifacts
- Significant performance slowdown

## Data Backup: Protect What Matters

Hardware can be replaced, but your data cannot. We've seen too many customers lose irreplaceable photos, documents, and business files.

### 3-2-1 Backup Rule

- **3** copies of your data
- **2** different storage types
- **1** off-site backup

### Practical Backup Solutions for Freetown

**Local Backups:**
- External hard drive (Le 400,000 - 800,000 for 1TB)
- USB flash drives for small files
- Second internal drive (desktops only)

**Cloud Backups:**
- Google Drive (15GB free)
- Microsoft OneDrive (5GB free)
- Dropbox (2GB free)

**Considerations for Sierra Leone:**
- Internet speed limits cloud backup options
- Upload important files gradually during fast internet times
- Encrypt sensitive business data
- Keep physical backups secure

### What to Backup

**Essential:**
- Documents, spreadsheets, presentations
- Photos and videos
- Business records and financial data
- Contact lists and emails

**Optional:**
- Music and movie collections
- Downloaded software installers
- Browser bookmarks

**Don't Need to Backup:**
- Windows system files (can be reinstalled)
- Installed programs (reinstall from source)
- Temporary internet files
